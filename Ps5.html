PS5 parts and info

console parts

  1. CPU: 8x Zen 2 Cores at 3.5GHz with SMT (variable frequency) vs 8x Jaguar Cores at 1.6Ghz on PS4.
  2. GPU: 10.28 teraflops with 36 compute units at 2.23GHz (variable frequency) vs 1.84 teraflops and 18 comput units at 800MHz on PS4.
  3. RAM: 16GB GDDR6/256-bit vs 8GB GDDR5 and 256-bit on PS4.
  4. Internal Storage: Custom 825GB SSD vs a 500GB HDD on PS4.
  5. Expandable Storage: NVMe SSD Slot
  6. Optical Drive: 4K UHD Blu-ray Drive vs standard Blu-ray Drive in the PS4.

For those who need a refresher, Mark Cerny is the Lead System Architect for Sony’s Playstation console. He designed the PS4 and, as we all knew in our hearts, he’s also been secretly working on the PS5 for the last several years. Today is the day, fellow gamers, as Mark Cerny himself sat down with Wired magazine to set the stage for the PS5 and reveal details that have only been mere rumors up until this point.The interview between Mark Cerny and Wired Magazine is our first public look at real concrete PS5 details. For starters, he confirmed that the console is not coming in 2019. This points towards our current PS5 release date prediction of November 2020.
